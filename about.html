My interest in teaching was inspired and created through my experiences I had while coaching within my youth rowing club. This position created ground work for me to go on to coach the NSW Junior team for 3 consecutive years and lead them to great sucess. The connection that i created with these kids and the difference i was able make to their lives was a driving choice behind my choice to pursue teaching. I knew that i had no interest in the convetional subjects of maths, english or science. I have always been a very hands on learner and completed creative or practical activites much easier compared to tasks like essays and research assignments. Industrial technology Teaching gives me the opportunity to fulfill my passion for creative or hands on work and my passion for coaching or helping individuals imporove their skills or understanding of a shared interest. My goal is to inspire students to improve their connection with learning and strengthen their critical and creative thinking skills, this lets them gain real-world skills that can be applied to any problem while also keeping the art of woodworking alive in a world of mass production..

My teaching Philosophy

My approach to teaching is different to most, Learning within industrial technology is for all intents and purposes a hands-on process, where students need to use all resources available to them. I view my role as a teacher as one of those resources. Students can be given all of the support and information available but if they cannot think critically to apply it then it is of no use. Teachers should encourage and demonstrate critical thinking in all lessons and a very simple but effectiv way of doing this is through challenging students ideas and constantly making them consider justifications for their processes. When students are taught this way analysing and improving designs and showing the iterative development becomes second nature. Knowing your students and their level of confidence to of the content is essential as you must work at their speed in order to properly develop both their technical skills and creative thinking. As rushing students can impact their ability to think creatively and negatively impact their processes.
